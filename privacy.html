Privacy & Data Stewardship

Your trust is the rarest spice in our pantry. At Yugen Gastronomy Cipher, we believe in radical transparency — no hidden ingredients, no data harvesting without purpose. This policy outlines how we collect, use, and protect the information you share when you explore our brown-themed universe.

Our commitment to confidentiality

We operate a small, independent culinary archive dedicated to umami, caramelization, and the poetry of earth-toned cooking. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Any data you provide — whether via contact form, newsletter sign-up (coming soon), or recipe correspondence — is treated with the same reverence as a slow-simmered broth.

Limited collection

We only collect information you voluntarily submit: name, email, message content, and optional phone number.

No third-party selling

Your data never enters data brokers or marketing lists. We don't share with advertisers — ever.

Data retention

Correspondence is retained for up to 24 months to resolve ongoing culinary inquiries, then anonymized.

1. Information we collect

Contact forms When you reach out via our contact page, we ask for your full name, email address, subject line, and message. You may optionally provide your phone number and preferred contact method. We also collect the timestamp and the nature of your request (e.g., recipe inquiry or press collaboration).

Usage data Like most websites, our servers may log standard technical information: IP address, browser type, referring pages, and date/time stamps. This data is anonymized within 30 days and used solely to understand traffic patterns and improve our site’s performance — not to identify individual visitors.

Newsletter & events (opt-in) In the future, should you subscribe to our “Brown Butter Dispatch” newsletter, we will collect only your email and name with explicit consent. You may unsubscribe at any moment through a one-click link.

2. Cookies & tracking technology

Yugen Gastronomy Cipher uses minimal essential cookies to enhance user experience: remembering your consent preferences, session management, and analytics via privacy-respecting tools. We do not deploy targeting or advertising cookies. For more details, please refer to our Cookies Policy.

You may disable cookies via your browser settings, but some interactive features may be affected. We respect Do Not Track signals where applicable.

3. How we use your information

  • Respond to your inquiries, recipe help, or collaboration proposals (our team reads every message 🍂).
  • Improve our recipe archives, user experience, and website accessibility based on aggregated feedback.
  • Send occasional operational updates (if you’ve opted-in or have active conversation threads).
  • Comply with legal obligations or protect against fraudulent activity — though we rarely encounter such cases.

4. Data sharing & third parties

We never sell your personal information. We may use limited third-party services to operate our website and manage correspondence:

  • Hosting provider: Secure servers located in the US (data resides within GDPR-respectful jurisdiction).
  • Email routing: Contact form submissions are sent via encrypted transmission to our private mailbox (no data stored on public servers longer than temporary routing).
  • Privacy-focused analytics: We may use aggregated analytics tools without storing personal identifiers. All IP addresses are masked.

Each processor is contractually bound to protect your data and process it solely on our behalf. We conduct quarterly reviews to ensure compliance.

5. Your rights (GDPR / CCPA / global privacy)

We respect your privacy rights, regardless of your location. If you are a resident of the European Economic Area, California, or any region with data protection laws, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Erasure: Ask us to delete your personal data, subject to limited exceptions (e.g., legal retention).
  • Restrict processing: Limit how we use your information.
  • Data portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Object to processing: Withdraw consent for marketing or opt out of any future communications.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Request team at privacy@yugengastronomy.com or via mail at Yugen Cipher — Privacy, 127 Brownstone Lane, Studio 4, Burlington, VT 05401. We respond within 30 days and never charge a fee for legitimate requests.

6. Children’s privacy

Our website and content are intended for a general audience interested in gastronomy, but we do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of 16. If you believe a child has provided us with data, please contact us immediately, and we will delete it.

7. Data security measures

We implement industry-standard safeguards: encrypted connections (SSL/TLS), limited access to personal data, regular security updates, and internal training on privacy best practices. While no online transmission is 100% secure, we continuously strive to protect your information against unauthorized access.

8. Changes to this privacy policy

From time to time, we may update this policy to reflect new practices or legal requirements. The “Last revised” date at the bottom of this page indicates when changes were made. Significant updates will be highlighted via a notice on our homepage or email to active correspondents. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

Short & sweet: We collect only what’s needed to reply to your culinary messages, never sell data, respect deletion requests, and keep your trust as our core ingredient.

Submit a privacy request

If you have any questions, wish to access or delete your data, or opt-out from any future contact, please use the form below. Alternatively, you can email us directly at privacy@yugengastronomy.com. We’ll confirm receipt within 3 business days.

For urgent Data Protection inquiries, you may also contact our designated representative via postal mail at the address above. We will never request sensitive data like payment information through this form.

Legal basis for processing (GDPR Art. 6): We process your data based on your consent (when submitting a form) and legitimate interest to respond to inquiries. You may withdraw consent at any time.
